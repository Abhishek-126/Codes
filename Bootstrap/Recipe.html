Pav Bhaji

Pav bhaji is a popular Indian street food dish originating from Maharashtra. It consists of a spicy vegetable mash (bhaji) typically made from potatoes, peas, tomatoes, and a variety of spices, served with buttered bread rolls (pav).

View Recipe
Recipe Name
Ingredients
  • Potatoes
  • Peas
  • Tomatoes
  • Bell peppers (capsicum)
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Green chilies (optional, for heat)
  • Pav bhaji masala (a spice blend)
  • Turmeric powder
  • Red chili powder
  • Cumin seeds
  • Butter or oil
  • Salt
  • Water
Instructions

1.Boil and Mash Potatoes: Peel and chop potatoes into cubes. Boil them until soft. Drain and mash them coarsely with a potato masher or fork.
2.Prepare Vegetables: Finely chop onions, tomatoes, bell peppers (capsicum), garlic, ginger, and green chilies.
3.Cooking: Heat a large pan or kadhai over medium heat and add butter or oil.
Cooking the Bhaji:
1.Tempering: Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Then add chopped garlic and green chilies. Saute until garlic turns golden.
2.Onions and Capsicum: Add chopped onions and bell peppers (capsicum). Cook until they turn soft and translucent.
3.Tomatoes: Add chopped tomatoes. Cook until tomatoes become soft and oil starts to separate from the mixture.
4.Spices: Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and pav bhaji masala. Mix well and cook for a minute to roast the spices.
5.Mashed Potatoes and Peas: Add the mashed potatoes and peas to the pan. Mix everything together thoroughly.
6.Mashing and Simmering: Use a potato masher to mash everything together while it simmers. Add water gradually to achieve the desired consistency. Season with salt to taste.
7.Simmer: Let the bhaji simmer on low heat for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally. This helps to blend the flavors together.
Preparing Pav:
1.Toasting Pav: Slit the pav rolls horizontally without cutting them completely into two parts. Heat a griddle or tava and toast the pav with a little butter until they are lightly browned and crisp on the surface.
Serving:
1.Plate the Bhaji: Transfer the hot bhaji to a serving plate. Garnish with chopped onions, chopped cilantro (coriander leaves), and a lemon wedge.
2.Serve: Serve hot pav bhaji with toasted pav rolls and additional butter on the side.
Enjoy your delicious pav bhaji!
